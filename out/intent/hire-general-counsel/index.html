How to Hire a General Counsel in the UK

A practical guide to General Counsel executive search in the UK — salary benchmarks, search fees, timelines, and what to look for in a search firm.

The General Counsel is the most senior legal officer in the organization, responsible for legal strategy, risk management, compliance, and corporate governance. For high-growth, regulated, or complex businesses, the GC is a critical strategic advisor to the CEO and board — not just a service function.

The UK market for General Counsel talent is relationship-driven and specialized. The best candidates combine deep legal expertise with commercial awareness, have operated credibly at board level, and understand how to balance legal risk with commercial opportunity.

Key Considerations When Hiring a General Counsel

  1. Define whether you need in-house legal leadership for the first time or replacing an existing GC

  2. Clarify scope: pure legal, or legal + compliance, risk, company secretarial, data protection?

  3. Be clear about regulatory environment and compliance requirements specific to your sector

  4. Establish board expectations — is this a strategic advisor role or operational legal head?

  5. Consider whether sector-specific legal expertise (financial services, healthcare, technology) is required

Salary Range

£95k–£240k depending on complexity, sector, and regulatory environment

Search Fee

28–33%

10–14 weeks

Why Use Executive Search for This Role?

The best General Counsels are not actively seeking roles. They are approached for opportunities where their specific legal expertise is directly relevant and where they can operate as strategic advisors, not just legal service providers. A specialist headhunter maps the market for candidates with the right sector, regulatory, and commercial profile — and structures an assessment that tests both legal capability and board-level credibility.

Key Responsibilities

  • Providing legal strategy, advice, and risk management to CEO and board

  • Managing all legal matters including contracts, litigation, disputes, and regulatory compliance

  • Leading corporate governance, company secretarial, and board support functions

  • Overseeing compliance programs and regulatory relationships

  • Managing intellectual property, data protection, and privacy matters

  • Supporting M&A, fundraising, and major commercial transactions

  • Building and managing legal team and external legal advisor relationships

  • Advising on employment law, commercial contracts, and corporate structure

Key Qualities to Look For

Legal Expertise

Deep legal knowledge across commercial, corporate, regulatory, and compliance areas

Commercial Judgment

Ability to balance legal risk with commercial opportunity and business objectives

Strategic Advisory

Can operate credibly as strategic advisor to CEO and board, not just legal service provider

Regulatory Knowledge

Understanding of relevant regulatory environment and compliance requirements

Risk Management

Strong judgment on legal risk assessment and mitigation strategies

Communication

Can explain complex legal matters clearly to non-legal stakeholders

Leadership

Track record of building and managing legal teams and external advisor relationships

Sector Experience

Relevant sector knowledge (particularly for regulated industries)

Interview Questions to Ask

  1. Walk me through the most complex legal or regulatory challenge you have navigated. What was your approach and what was the outcome?

  2. Describe a situation where you had to balance legal risk against commercial opportunity. How did you advise and what happened?

  3. Tell me about your experience building and managing an in-house legal function. What did you focus on and what was the impact?

  4. How do you manage relationships with external legal advisors? Give me examples of when you have used external counsel effectively.

  5. Describe your approach to regulatory compliance and risk management. What frameworks have you implemented?

  6. Tell me about a time when you had to deliver difficult legal advice to the CEO or board. How did you approach it?

  7. Walk me through your experience with M&A or major transactions. What was your role and what legal risks did you manage?

  8. How do you ensure the legal function adds commercial value beyond risk management? Give specific examples.

  9. Describe your experience with data protection, privacy, and cybersecurity legal matters. What have you implemented?

  10. What would your first 90 days look like here? How would you assess legal risk and prioritize legal function development?

Step-by-Step Hiring Guide

1

Define the GC Mandate

Be clear whether you need strategic legal advisor to board, operational legal head, or first in-house legal leadership. Different profiles and different assessment criteria.

2

Clarify Scope and Structure

Will the GC own compliance, risk, company secretarial, data protection, or just pure legal? Define reporting lines and scope clearly.

3

Assess Legal Depth

Test legal expertise through case studies or legal scenarios relevant to your business. Look for candidates with specific sector or regulatory experience if required.

4

Evaluate Commercial Judgment

Strong GCs balance legal risk with commercial opportunity. Test their ability to provide pragmatic, commercial legal advice — not just risk aversion.

5

Test Board-Level Credibility

Will this person command respect with your board and investor base? Assess their ability to communicate complex legal matters clearly to non-legal stakeholders.

6

Check Regulatory Knowledge

For regulated industries, test regulatory and compliance knowledge rigorously. Weak regulatory advice is a material business risk.

7

Include CEO and Board Interviews

Have shortlisted candidates meet with CEO and board members. The GC must be trusted advisor to both.

8

Plan Legal Onboarding

First 90 days: legal risk audit, regulatory compliance review, contract and litigation review, external advisor assessment, legal function development plan.
